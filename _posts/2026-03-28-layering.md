Cold hands and a sweat-soaked back are both failures of planning, not weather. The fix is a
layering system you can adjust at a gas stop in under two minutes.

<!--more-->

## The three layers

1. **Base** — merino or synthetic that moves moisture off your skin. Never cotton.
2. **Mid** — a light insulator you can add or shed. This is your thermostat.
3. **Shell** — wind and waterproof, with vents that actually open.

## The wind is the enemy

At highway speed a 60°F day feels like 45°F. Windproofing matters more than raw insulation,
which is why a thin windproof layer beats a thick fleece nearly every time.

## Extremities

Heated grips change everything on a cold [Angeles Crest dawn patrol](/notes/routes/angeles-crest/).
For the coast, where fog can drop the temperature 20°F in a mile, this system is what makes
[PCH](/notes/routes/pch/) enjoyable instead of endured. Match it to a well-fitting
[helmet](/notes/gear/helmets/) and you'll ride longer and sharper.

> Dress for the coldest ten minutes, vent for the rest.
