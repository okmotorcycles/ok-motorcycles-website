There is a reason every list of great motorcycle roads eventually lands on **CA-1**. It is
not the fastest way anywhere. That is the point.

<!--more-->

## The plan

Run it north to south so the ocean is on your right and the pull-outs are on your side of
the road. Give it two days minimum. One long day turns the best coastline in the country
into a blur of aching wrists.

- **Day one:** Leggett to Monterey. Redwoods, then the opening act of the coast.
- **Day two:** Monterey through Big Sur to Morro Bay. This is the postcard.

## Riding it well

Big Sur is a series of second- and third-gear corners with a cliff on one side and tourists
braking mid-apex on the other. Slow in, look through, and leave room. If you have not
already, read [Trail Braking, Explained Slowly](/notes/craft/trail-braking/) before you go —
it is the single skill that makes this road relax instead of clench.

> The fog will roll in around 4pm whether you are ready or not. Pack for it.

## What to bring

Coastal weather swings 25°F in an afternoon. Layers are not optional here — see
[Layering for the Shoulder Seasons](/notes/gear/layering/). When you need to stop and let
your hands recover, [Cooling Down: Rest Stops That Matter](/notes/routes/rest-stops/) has
the pull-outs worth the detour.

If you only ride one road in California, ride this one. Then ride
[Angeles Crest](/notes/routes/angeles-crest/) to remember what corners feel like.
