Riding in a group is a different skill from riding alone. The road is the same; the mistakes
are different. Most of them come from one thing: riding someone else's ride instead of your
own.

<!--more-->

## The staggered formation

On straight, open road, ride staggered — leader in the left third, next rider in the right
third, back to the left, and so on. It buys everyone two seconds of following distance while
keeping the group tight. In corners, everyone takes the **full lane** and their own line.

## The rules that keep it safe

- **Ride your own pace.** The group regroups at every turn and every stop. Nobody is left.
- **Second position sets the pace,** not the leader — so the front doesn't drag the back
  into the weeds.
- **Fuel range is the group's range.** Plan around the smallest tank.

## Ego is the hazard

The temptation to keep up is exactly how people end up braking mid-corner, which is the
worst moment to grab a handful. If you feel that pressure, back off and let your
[trail braking](/notes/craft/trail-braking/) do the work at *your* pace.

> The goal is that everyone gets the coffee stop. All of them.
