Everything else in the gear closet is a trade-off. The helmet is not. Buy the best-fitting
one you can afford, replace it after a crash, and never buy one used.

<!--more-->

## Fit is the whole game

A helmet one size too big is a helmet that isn't there when you need it. It should be snug
enough that moving the shell moves your skin, with no pressure points after twenty minutes.
Cheeks compress and break in; the crown does not.

## Reading the certifications

- **DOT** — the legal minimum in the US.
- **ECE 22.06** — a modern, rigorous standard. Look for it.
- **SNELL** — additional testing, popular on track.

## Full face, always

Chin bars exist because a huge share of impacts land on the chin. A modular is a reasonable
compromise for touring; an open face is a haircut with a strap. Pair the right lid with the
right layers — see [Layering for the Shoulder Seasons](/notes/gear/layering/) — and you can
ride comfortably in far more weather than you'd think.

> The rule is simple: all the gear, all the time. The helmet is where it starts.
