I bought a running-ish **1976 Honda CB550** for the price of a nice set of tires. This note
is the live log — **under grading** and updated as the build goes, mistakes and all.

<!--more-->

## Where it started

Four cylinders, four carbs, a tank of varnish, and a wiring harness that had clearly been
"improved" by a previous owner with a spool of speaker wire.

## The teardown list

- **Carbs:** full strip, ultrasonic clean, new jets and o-rings. Bench-sync before install.
- **Top end:** while it's apart, do the [valve clearances](/notes/maintenance/valve-clearance/)
  properly — this bike has clearly never had them checked.
- **Electrical:** rip the speaker wire, new harness, LED signals, keep it simple.
- **Ergonomics:** clip-ons, rearsets, a brat-style seat hoop.

## The plan, roughly

1. Get it running *right* on the stock setup first. No cosmetics until it runs.
2. Sort the [chain and sprockets](/notes/maintenance/chain-ritual/) — the current chain is a
   rust sculpture.
3. Then, and only then, make it pretty.

> The fastest way to never finish a build is to paint the tank first. Mechanicals earn the paint.

Next update: carbs on the bench and hopefully a first startup.
