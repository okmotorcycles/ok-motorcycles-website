The splash screen says it plainly: **motorcycles are OK**. Not great, not the answer to
everything, not a personality. Just OK — in the way a thing can be exactly enough.

<!--more-->

## Attention as a feature

A car lets you leave. A motorcycle makes you arrive. You cannot check out on a bike; the
road demands the whole of you, and in exchange it gives back an afternoon where you thought
about nothing but the next corner. That trade is the entire pitch.

## OK is the right amount

We are suspicious of things that promise transcendence. A motorcycle promises a commute that
doesn't feel like one, a Sunday that feels longer than it is, and a reason to learn how a
machine works. That is a modest, honest offer. It is OK, and OK is plenty.

## Where to start

If you're new, start with the one non-negotiable — a [proper helmet](/notes/gear/helmets/) —
and then go ride [Pacific Coast Highway](/notes/routes/pch/) slowly, with the fog coming in,
and see if you get it.

> Ride within your limits. Wave at the other riders. Leave the road better than a car would.
