A chain is the cheapest part that can ruin the most expensive day. Give it twenty minutes
every 500–600 miles and it will give you 20,000 miles back.

<!--more-->

## The ritual

1. **Warm it up.** A short ride, or a few minutes on a rear stand rolling the wheel.
2. **Clean.** Kerosene or a proper chain cleaner and a grunge brush. No wire brushes.
3. **Inspect.** Spin slowly and look for tight spots, kinked links, and hooked teeth.
4. **Lube.** Apply to the *inside* of the chain, let it sit, wipe the excess.
5. **Adjust.** Set free play to spec, check alignment on both sides.

## Reading the wear

If a section is tight while the rest is loose, the chain is worn unevenly and it is on its
way out. While you have the bike on the stand, this is the perfect moment to also go
[read your tires](/notes/maintenance/reading-tires/) — same stand, same ten minutes.

> A dry chain is a countdown. A drowned chain flings lube on your tire. Aim for the middle.

## When to replace

When free play won't hold, when the rear axle is near the end of its adjustment, or when
the sprocket teeth start to hook. Replace chain and both sprockets together, always.
