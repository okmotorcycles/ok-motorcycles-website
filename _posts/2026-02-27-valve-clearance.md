Valve clearances are the maintenance item people quietly ignore until the bike runs hot,
idles rough, or burns a valve. This note is **under grading** — I'm writing it as I do the
job on the [CB550 build](/notes/builds/cb550-cafe/).

<!--more-->

## Why it matters

As valves and seats wear, clearances *tighten*. Too tight and a valve can't fully close,
loses its cooling path to the head, and eventually burns. Checking is cheap. Ignoring is a
top-end rebuild.

## The tools

- A set of feeler gauges, thin ones especially.
- The correct clearance spec for intake and exhaust.
- Patience, and the ability to find true top-dead-center.

## The process (short version)

1. Cold engine. Always.
2. Rotate to TDC on the compression stroke for the cylinder you're checking.
3. Slide the correct feeler gauge — you want a light, even drag.
4. Adjust, torque the locknut, re-check.

right spec, light drag. Don't fear it.
