Tires are the only part of the motorcycle that touches the road. They also keep a detailed
diary of how you ride and how you maintain the bike. Learn to read it.

<!--more-->

## Pressure first

Cold pressure, checked weekly, with your own gauge. Gas-station gauges lie. Low pressure
makes a bike feel vague and eats the shoulders; high pressure makes it skittish and eats the
center.

## The wear patterns

- **Flat center strip** — lots of highway, likely over-inflated. Common on tourers.
- **Cupping / scalloping** — worn suspension or chronic low pressure.
- **Worn shoulders, fresh center** — you, apparently, having a good time in the canyons.

## Depth and age

Check the wear bars, but also the date code. A tire that is five years old is done even if
it looks fine — the rubber hardens and grip falls off a cliff. When you find worn shoulders
here, it usually pairs with a chain that needs the [600-mile ritual](/notes/maintenance/chain-ritual/),
and it is a good sign your [trail braking](/notes/craft/trail-braking/) is coming along.

> Your tires are honest. Listen to them before they get loud.
