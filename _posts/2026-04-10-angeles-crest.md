**CA-2** climbs out of La Cañada and does not stop climbing for an hour. On a Saturday
afternoon it is a parade of cars, cyclists, and cruisers. At 5:30am it is yours.

<!--more-->

## Why dawn

The Crest rewards rhythm, and rhythm needs empty road. Early light also means cold
pavement, so the first twenty minutes are a warm-up lap, not a time trial.

## The sections

1. **The switchbacks** — tight, technical, cold in the shade. Patient throttle.
2. **Newcomb's stretch** — flowing sweepers where the road finally opens up.
3. **The high desert side** — long sightlines, big skies, watch for gravel wash.

## Respect the mountain

Rangers close sections after storms and the surface changes week to week. Sand in a corner
does not care how good you are. Everything on the Crest comes down to
[trail braking](/notes/craft/trail-braking/) and reading the surface — the same habits that
keep [Pacific Coast Highway](/notes/routes/pch/) relaxed keep the Crest survivable.

> Ride it like you'll ride it again next weekend. Because that is the whole idea.
